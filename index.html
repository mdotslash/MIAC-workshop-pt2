The Opportunity

Building Artist-Powered Digital Collections & Cultural Archives

This March, join California-based AMPL Labs in Santa Fe, New Mexico for a digital cultural heritage professional development intensive for artists interested in working with communities and cultural institutions to preserve and protect cultural knowledge.

The intensive will focus on using accessible 3D scanning technology and open-source digital collections as medium for restorative infrastructure and innovative cultural collaboration.

Artists will also learn how to effectively collaborate with institutions to ensure community heritage, intellectual propery is not just "stored" but thrives through active participation and agency.

This training is offered by award-winning artist Mayowa Tomori (AMPL Labs) and Indigenous creative technologist Monica Silva Lovato.

Training Focus

  • Technical Mastery of 3D Scanning: Learn how to create high-fidelity 3D models using photogrammetry.
  • Museum Technology Integration: Learn how to create mixed reality 3D archives using Smithsonian Voyager and other Open Source tools.
  • Ethics & Working with Institutions: How to minimize harm, take care of your team, community while working with collections.
  • Business Strategy: How to build sustainable business partnerships with cultural instution, fund collaborative research.
  • Activating Collections: Learn how to adopt archival research into your creative practice.

The Host Institution

Museum of Indian Arts & Culture

MIAC is a premier institution in Santa Fe dedicated to the native arts, histories, and languages of the Greater Southwest. As a Smithsonian-affiliated museum, it serves as the primary laboratory for our intensive.

Participants gain rare, direct access to collections, working alongside staff archivists to explore the intersection of material culture and digital sovereignty protocols.
