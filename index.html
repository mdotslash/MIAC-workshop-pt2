The Opportunity

Resilient Community Archives

This March, join California-based AMPL Labs in Santa Fe for a high-level professional development intensive designed to bridge the gap between creative practice and the museum technology industry. Led by Mayowa Tomori (permanent collection, Smithsonian Design Museum) and Indigenous technologist Monica Silva Lovato, this workshop transforms 3D scanning from a technical tool into a medium for cultural sovereignty and restorative infrastructure.

In an era where cultural heritage is under constant threat of erasure, artists must learn to navigate and collaborate with institutions to create more resilient community archives. This training empowers artists to leverage museum resources to protect their own stories—ensuring that community heritage is not just "stored" but thrives through Restorative Infrastructure.

A Professional Development Training Intensive

Gain the technical skills and business "know-how" to translate your creative practice into a sustainable revenue stream. This training will help you:

  • Technical Mastery: Learn high-fidelity 3D scanning using accessible tools.
  • Museum Integration: Understand how to publish models for AR/Web experiences (Smithsonian Voyager).
  • Business Strategy: Position yourself as a vendor and price your services ($2,000+).
  • Direct Networking: Connect with professionals at a premier Smithsonian-affiliated institution.
